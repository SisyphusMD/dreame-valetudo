# Self-generated-key root of an Allwinner A133 / MR813 Dreame — research compendium

This directory is a durable, self-contained record of an attempt to boot custom firmware
(Valetudo) on a "gen3" Dreame robot vacuum using **signing keys generated by the device owner**,
rather than the vendor's fleet signing key or a vendor-key-signed image from a build service.

It exists so the work does not have to be re-derived. Every reverse-engineered address, the boot
chain, the flash-authorization scheme, the boot0 write/read pipeline, the eFuse read protocol, the
signature formats, the UART behaviour, and the exact point where the attempt is blocked are written
down here in full. The reproduction tooling (source) and a hash manifest of every binary artifact
are included; the binaries themselves are not (see [Artifacts](#artifacts)).

## Intent

This is de-clouding research on hardware the owner physically possesses, over a local USB cable,
with no networks or third-party systems involved: replacing cloud-dependent vendor firmware with
Valetudo (the established open-source local-control firmware) so the robot runs entirely on the
local network. It is the bootloader-level equivalent of unlocking a phone you own. It is the same
class of work published openly by the Valetudo and dustbuilder communities
([valetudo.cloud](https://valetudo.cloud), [builder.dontvacuum.me](https://builder.dontvacuum.me)).

## The one-paragraph result

The chain that boots a genuine unit is `BROM → toc0 (boot0/SPL) → toc1 (u-boot/OP-TEE/… + kernel &
rootfs hash pins) → Linux`. Each stage verifies the next. A **correctly self-signed toc0 is rejected
by the mask-ROM BROM before the SPL ever prints its banner**, and a **correctly re-signed toc1 is
rejected by boot0 before u-boot loads**. The reject anchor was localized by disassembly to a single
thing: the **eFuse ROTPK** (root-of-trust public key) burned into the SoC. On an unburned part boot0
accepts any key; on a burned part it accepts only the key whose hash matches the fuse. The reference
unit's fuse **reads all-zero over ~100 non-destructive reads, but that region is plausibly
secure-read-masked**, so all-zero is not proof of "empty." The decisive evidence is indirect but
strong: a **byte-perfect, cryptographically valid self-signed toc0 was still rejected**, which a
truly accept-any BROM could not do — so the fuse is, in effect, **burned to the vendor's key**.
Everything short of possessing the vendor's private key was achieved.

## What was achieved (hardware-validated)

- FEL entry and RAM-loaded fastboot over a custom libusb client (see `02`).
- The `oem dust` flash-authorization token derived from first principles (see `03`).
- Raw **boot0/toc0 write** to both on-device slots, plus a **read-back verify oracle** that defeats
  the transport-layer obfuscation on the dump path (see `04`).
- A **non-destructive eFuse ROTPK read** replicating boot0's own read protocol over FEL (see `05`).
- Full RE of the **toc0 signature scheme** and a byte-perfect re-sign (see `06`).
- Full RE of the **SPL's verification logic** — the actual wall (see `07`).
- Full RE of **toc1's structure** and a structurally-correct re-sign of all seven certs (see `08`).
- A complete map of the **u-boot/OP-TEE key gate** downstream (built as a candidate wall, then
  hardware-refuted — see `09`).
- Reliable **recovery** to the genuine chain at every step (BROM sits below toc0).

## Artifacts

- **`tools/`** — the reproduction tooling, as source (sanitized). This is the concrete "how", and it
  leans on sister tooling already shipped in this repository (`dreame_valetudo/`,
  `libexec/fastboot-libusb.py`).
- **`artifacts/`** — text only: the annotated UART capture, re-sign provenance records, and the
  reproducible toc0 byte-diff.
- **`artifacts/MANIFEST.md`** — every binary artifact (self-signed images, keys, extracted genuine
  firmware, RAM payloads) listed by SHA-256 with a regeneration recipe. **No firmware, keys, or
  images are stored here** — the manifest lets a future reader rebuild or re-identify each one.

## Scope of the target

"gen3" here means the Allwinner **A133** (`sun50iw10`, marketed MR813) **fastboot family** — the
`r`-prefix Dreame models (X40 `r2416`, D10S Plus `r2240`, D10S Pro `r2250`, L10s `r2228`/`r2338`,
…). The reference unit throughout is a **D10S Plus (`r2240`)**. The older `p`/`mc`-prefix UART
serial-shell family (e.g. Z10 `p2028`) is a different platform and out of scope.
